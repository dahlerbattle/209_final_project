    • Are vaccinations safe?

    As of December 1st, 2021, 40% of the US population is not fully vaccinated. There is much debate over the efficacy and safety of the vaccine itself, so we offer the opportunity to explore some of the more concerning data and objectively explore the dangers of COVID-19 against the possible dangers of the vaccine.

    We'll look at three separate datasets in this exploration. To view covid deaths, we'll use the CDC's COVID-19 Deaths by Week, Sex, and Age dataset, to take a deep dive into vaccination rates, we'll use the CDC's COVID-19 Vaccinations in the United States dataset, and to explore alleged adverse effects from the vaccine, we'll use the US Dept of Health & Human Services Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS) dataset. It's worth noting that the VAERS dataset is not new- it's been around since 1990 to be used as an early indicator for any potentially dangerous vaccination side effects, and it is a completely voluntary system. Any individual could falsely report an adverse reaction, and that false information would forever stay in the database. We absolutely encourage you to read the disclaimer on their website to understand the limitations of the dataset.

    Even with these limitaions in the dataset, we chose to explore VAERS because it's been widely touted as proof that the vaccine is indeed dangerous. Not ones to take another's word for it, we decided to dig in on our own, and share our findings with you!
